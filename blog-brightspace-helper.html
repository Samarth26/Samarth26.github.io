Learning Management System RAG Helper

Edge AI RAG Small Language Models Chrome Extension

Overview

Managing course materials often involves endlessly downloading PDF slides, assignments, and readings, then manually searching through them to find specific information. I am building a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) solution powerd by Google's Gemma 2 2B for inference and Qwen 3 for embeddings. This allows students to efficiently retrieve course information and generate notes without the manual hassle.

The core concept is simple: Drag and drop your course files, and the RAG engine immediately indexes them. You can then ask questions, and the LLM generates answers backed by the specific content in your files.

The Workflow

The solution offers scalable storage options designed for efficiency:

  • Drag & Drop Interface: Users simply drag their course documents (PDFs, PPTs, DOCX) into the application.
  • Google Drive Integration: For seamless access, users can link their Google Drive accounts to import course materials directly.
  • MongoDB Atlas Integration: For scalability, the system supports a Vector API backed by MongoDB Atlas, enabling faster retrieval over larger document sets.
  • Instant Retrieval: The RAG system retrieves the top relevant chunks (default: 8 chunks) to answer student queries in real-time.

Edge AI & Privacy

This is an Edge AI solution focused on privacy. Users can choose between cloud-based inference via Hugging Face or complete local execution.

For maximum privacy, the tool supports Ollama integration. This allows you to run models like Llama 3.2 3B entirely on your own device, ensuring that your course notes and files never leave your machine.

Future Roadmap: Agentic Workflows

While the current version relies on user-initiated drag-and-drop or Drive integration, the future roadmap involves significant agentic automation.

I will be adding agents capable of:

  • Direct Brightspace Integration: The model will autonomously "look" at files on Brightspace, identifying new materials as they are posted.
  • Auto-Ingestion: Agents will pick up these files, index them, and update the knowledge base without any user intervention.
  • Proactive Summarization: Generating weekly summaries or study guides automatically as new content becomes available.
