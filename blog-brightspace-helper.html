Brightspace RAG Helper

Edge AI RAG Small LLMs Chrome Extension

Overview

Managing course materials often involves endlessly downloading PDF slides, assignments, and readings, then manually searching through them to find specific information. I am building a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) solution using Small LLMs that allows students to efficiently retrieve course information and generate notes without the manual hassle.

The core concept is simple: Drag and drop your course files, and the RAG engine immediately indexes them. You can then ask questions, and the LLM generates answers backed by the specific content in your files.

The Workflow

The solution is designed for friction-free access to knowledge. Instead of navigating complex folder structures or re-downloading files:

  • Drag & Drop Interface: Users simply drag their course documents (PDFs, PPTs, DOCX) into the application.
  • Google Drive Integration: Files can be saved to and retrieved directly from Google Drive, eliminating the need to keep local copies or repeatedly download materials from the learning management system.
  • Instant Retrieval: The RAG system efficiently retrieves relevant chunks of information from the indexed documents to answer student queries in real-time.

Edge AI & Privacy

This is an Edge AI solution. By utilizing Small LLMs (like Llama-7B via local inference or optimized quantized models), the goal is to keep processing efficiently on the client side or with minimal latency.

Processing data locally or "at the edge" ensures that sensitive course data and personal notes remain private and secure, while also reducing the costs associated with large cloud-based model inference.

Future Roadmap: Agentic Workflows

While the current version relies on user-initiated drag-and-drop or Drive integration, the future roadmap involves significant agentic automation.

I will be adding agents capable of:

  • Direct Brightspace Integration: The model will autonomously "look" at files on Brightspace, identifying new materials as they are posted.
  • Auto-Ingestion: Agents will pick up these files, index them, and update the knowledge base without any user intervention.
  • Proactive Summarization: Generating weekly summaries or study guides automatically as new content becomes available.
